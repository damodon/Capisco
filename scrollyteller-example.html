Q4 by Region
All four regions hit Q4 targets. Total revenue: $15.1M.
Section 01

Strong gains across every region

Q4 2025 saw consistent outperformance across NAM, EMEA, APAC, and LATAM. All four regions hit or exceeded their targets — a first in company history.

Total Q4 revenue landed at $15.1M, representing a 22% increase over Q4 2024.

APAC — Record Quarter

APAC breaks all records

APAC posted $5.8M in Q4 revenue — a 41% year-on-year increase and the region's best quarter ever. This was driven by two enterprise deals closed in late November.

+41% APAC YoY growth
EMEA — Contract Spike

A single deal moved EMEA's needle

EMEA's Q4 total of $3.1M includes a $2.1M contract renewal that closed in November. Strip that out and underlying growth was a more modest 9%.

The renewal confirms our enterprise motion in EMEA — the team is now targeting three similar accounts for Q1.

⚠️ Renewal dependency — watch Q1
NAM — Steady Foundation

NAM: the reliable engine

NAM contributed $4.2M — an 8% increase YoY, steady and predictable. While less dramatic than APAC, this represents a healthy, compounding growth trajectory across a mature market.

New business in NAM now accounts for 38% of bookings, up from 28% in Q3.

Channel Breakdown

Three channels, one record quarter

Q4 2025 saw all three revenue channels hit new highs simultaneously — a first. Direct led by volume, Partner led by growth rate, and Self-serve crossed a symbolic milestone.

Direct Sales

Direct beats its own forecast

Direct sales contributed $7.3M — 48% of total Q4 revenue and the channel's best share in three years. Enterprise deal sizes grew 22% as buyers consolidated spend.

+22% deal size YoY
Partner Channel

Partner revenue surges 62%

Three new reseller agreements signed in September began generating revenue in October. Partner-led ARR is now $4.6M — up from $2.8M in Q3.

🤝 3 new resellers active
Self-Serve

Self-serve crosses $1M ARR

Product-led growth is working. Self-serve ARR crossed $1M for the first time in Q4, with 340 new accounts activated without any sales touch.

Section 02

October surge, then steady climb

The quarter opened with a strong October spike driven by two overlapping campaign launches. Use the controls to switch metric or adjust smoothing.

October

Campaigns fired up the pipeline

Week one of October saw a +18% surge in new pipeline — the direct result of the APAC-targeted outbound campaign and a product launch webinar.

Revenue lagged slightly as deals took 3–4 weeks to close, appearing in the weekly tally from Oct 15 onward.

November

Plateau — but healthy

November saw revenue stabilise after the promotional period ended. This is normal — a short plateau following a campaign is healthy and reduces churn risk.

The EMEA renewal closed Nov 8, appearing as a spike in week two. Underlying new business held flat at ~$340K per week.

December

Strong close — beat forecast

December closed at $5.2M — 6% ahead of forecast. The final two weeks showed accelerating deal closures as buyers pushed to spend remaining budget before year-end.

Looking ahead

What this means for Q1 2026

The Q4 momentum gives us a strong pipeline heading into 2026. APAC will be the primary growth target, with EMEA as a high-potential secondary market bolstered by the November renewal relationship.

Product mix shifts toward higher-margin tiers — expect AOV to rise even if volume growth moderates. The enterprise motion is working; we should double down.
